Exercise  546： Let AC⊥HB and AD⊥DB. Given that A, H, D are collinear and CD//HB, prove that \(AD·HD=CD·HB\).
\(\because \) AD⊥DB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) AC⊥HB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{HB}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{BH} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=-①+②=0\)
\(\because\) A, H, D are collinear and CD//HB   \(\therefore\) \(AD·HD=CD·HB\).
Exercise  603： Let ACED be a parallelogram. CA⊥HB and HD⊥DA. Given that C, H, D are collinear and E, B, D are collinear, prove that CD\(\cdot\)HD=BD\(\cdot\)ED.
\(\because \) ACED is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DE}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) HD⊥DA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) CA⊥HB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{HB}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=- \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=①+②=0\)
\(\because\) C, H, D are collinear and E, B, D are collinear   \(\therefore\) CD\(\cdot\)HD=BD\(\cdot\)ED.
Exercise  630： Let E be the midpoint of HF and BD. CA⊥HB and CD⊥DB. Given that C, H, D are collinear and A, D, F are collinear, prove that CD\(\cdot\)HD=AD\(\cdot\)DF.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of HF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DF}=2 \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}=\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}\).
\(\because \) CD⊥DB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) CA⊥HB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{HB}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=①+②=0\)
\(\because\) C, H, D are collinear and A, D, F are collinear   \(\therefore\) CD\(\cdot\)HD=AD\(\cdot\)DF.
