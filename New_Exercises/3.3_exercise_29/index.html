Exercise  37： Let BFAD be a trapezoid with BD//FA and BD=2FA. H, G are the midpoints of BC, CD, respectively. FA⊥AE. Given that F, H, E are collinear and EG//HA, prove that \(EG·HA=FH·HE\).
\(\because \) BD//FA and BD=2FA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AD}=\small\overrightarrow{AB} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{AF}\).
\(\because \) G is the midpoint of CD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AG}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AD}}{2}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AF}\).
\(\because \) H is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AH}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) FA⊥AE  \( \therefore\small\overrightarrow{AE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AF}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{AH} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EG} + \small\overrightarrow{EH} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{FH}=- \small\overrightarrow{AH} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AE} + \small\overrightarrow{AG}\right) + \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AE} + \small\overrightarrow{AH}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AF} + \small\overrightarrow{AH}\right)=- \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AE} - \small\overrightarrow{AF}\right) + \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AE}\right) \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AF}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{AE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AF}=0\)
\(\because\) F, H, E are collinear and EG//HA   \(\therefore\) \(EG·HA=FH·HE\).
Exercise  46： Let GAFB be a trapezoid with GB//AF and GB=2AF. H, D are the midpoints of CG, CB, respectively. FA⊥AE and FD⊥DE. Prove that HE⊥AD.
\(\because \) D is the midpoint of CB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) GB//AF and GB=2AF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BG}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BF}\).
\(\because \) H is the midpoint of CG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BH}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BG}}{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BF}\).
\(\because \) FD⊥DE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{ED} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{FD}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{BD} - \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{BD} - \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=\left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right) \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}}{4} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) FA⊥AE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AF}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE} - \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF} + \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EH}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BE} + \small\overrightarrow{BH}\right)=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BE} - \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE} + \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}}{4} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF}}{2}=①-②=0\), that is, HE⊥AD.
