Exercise  48： Let EABC be a parallelogram. DB=AD and ED=AB. Prove that \(AC^{2}=CB^{2} + DC^{2}\).
\(\because \) EABC is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BE}=\small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BC}\).
\(\because \) DB=AD  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) ED=AB  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DE}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=-①-②=0\), that is, \(AC^{2}=CB^{2} + DC^{2}\).
Exercise  54： Let E be the midpoint of AC and FD. FB=AB and DB=AD. Prove that \(AC^{2}=CB^{2} + DC^{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of FD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BF}=- \small\overrightarrow{BD} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BE}=\small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}\).
\(\because \) DB=AD  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) FB=AB  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=-①-②=0\), that is, \(AC^{2}=CB^{2} + DC^{2}\).
Exercise  81： Let E be the midpoint of AC and DF. AC=BA and BD=DC. Prove that \(BF^{2}=AD^{2} + BC^{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of DF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CF}=- \small\overrightarrow{CD} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\).
\(\because \) AC=BA  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BD=DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2} + \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2} + \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CF}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=-①-②=0\), that is, \(BF^{2}=AD^{2} + BC^{2}\).
Exercise  121： Let DAEC be a parallelogram. AC=DC and DB=BC. Prove that \(EB^{2}=AB^{2} + AD^{2}\).
\(\because \) DAEC is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\).
\(\because \) AC=DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) DB=BC  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} + \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}=- \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}\right)^{2} + \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CE}\right)^{2}=- \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}\right)^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=-①-②=0\), that is, \(EB^{2}=AB^{2} + AD^{2}\).
Exercise  196： Let E be the midpoint of AC and FB. FD=AD and BD=AB. Prove that \(AC^{2}=BC^{2} + CD^{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of FB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DF}=- \small\overrightarrow{DB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DE}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) FD=AD  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DF}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BD=AB  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=-①-②=0\), that is, \(AC^{2}=BC^{2} + CD^{2}\).
Exercise  282： Let AECB be a parallelogram. ED=DC and AC=CB. Prove that \(DB^{2}=AB^{2} + AD^{2}\).
\(\because \) AECB is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DE}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) ED=DC  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DE}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) AC=CB  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right)^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB}=-①-②=0\), that is, \(DB^{2}=AB^{2} + AD^{2}\).
