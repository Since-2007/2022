Exercise  34： Let B be the midpoint of FC and AG. FA⊥AE and FG⊥GE. Prove that CE⊥AG.
\(\because \) B is the midpoint of AG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DG}}{2}\).
\(\because \) B is the midpoint of FC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DC}=2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DF}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\).
\(\because \) FG⊥GE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{GE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GF}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} - \small\overrightarrow{DF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) FA⊥AE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AF}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DE}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DF}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AG} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DE}\right)=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} + \small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} + \small\overrightarrow{DF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}^{2}=-①+②=0\), that is, CE⊥AG.
Exercise  36： Let C, B be the midpoints of FE, AG, respectively. FA⊥AE and FG⊥GE. Prove that BC⊥AG.
\(\because \) B is the midpoint of AG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DG}}{2}\).
\(\because \) C is the midpoint of FE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DC}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DF}}{2}\).
\(\because \) FG⊥GE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{GE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GF}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} - \small\overrightarrow{DF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) FA⊥AE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AF}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DE}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DF}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AG} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DF}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DG}}{2}\right)=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DG}^{2}}{2}=-1/2\cdot①+1/2\cdot②=0\), that is, BC⊥AG.
Exercise  40： Let B be the midpoint of EF and CG. CA⊥AG and EG⊥GF. Prove that EA⊥AF.
\(\because \) B is the midpoint of EF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DF}}{2}\).
\(\because \) B is the midpoint of CG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DC}=2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}=\small\overrightarrow{DE} + \small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\).
\(\because \) EG⊥GF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{GE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GF}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} - \small\overrightarrow{DF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) CA⊥AG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AG}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right)=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} + \small\overrightarrow{DF} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} + \small\overrightarrow{DF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DG} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AF}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DE}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DF}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF}=①+②=0\), that is, EA⊥AF.
