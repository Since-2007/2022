Exercise  604： Let CADG be a parallelogram. D is the midpoint of HG. CA⊥AB. Given that C, D, B are collinear and HB//AD, prove that \(CD·DB=AD·HB\).
\(\because \) CADG is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DG}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) D is the midpoint of HG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DH}=- \small\overrightarrow{DG}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) CA⊥AB  \( \therefore\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{HB} + \small\overrightarrow{BD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\)
\(\because\) C, D, B are collinear and HB//AD   \(\therefore\) \(CD·DB=AD·HB\).
Exercise  676： Let DBAG be a trapezoid with BA//DG and BA=2DG. CEB is a triangle with CE⊥EB and EA⊥CB where A lies on CB. H is the midpoint of DC. BD⊥DC. Given that DA//GH, prove that \(AE^{2}=2 DA \cdot GH\).
\(\because \) BA//DG and BA=2DG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AG}=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{AD}\).
\(\because \) H is the midpoint of DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AH}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AD}}{2}\).
\(\because \) CEB is a triangle with CE⊥EB and EA⊥CB where A lies on CB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BD⊥DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{AB} + \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AC} + \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC} - \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD} - \small\overrightarrow{AC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(2 \small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GH} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AG} + \small\overrightarrow{AH}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{AE}^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AD}}{2}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{AE}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}^{2}=①-②=0\)
\(\because\) DA//GH   \(\therefore\) \(AE^{2}=2 DA \cdot GH\).
Exercise  24531： Let G be the midpoint of AH and BC. AD⊥DH and BD⊥DC. Prove that BA⊥AC.
\(\because \) G is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DG}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) G is the midpoint of AH  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DH}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DG}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) AD⊥DH  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DH}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BD⊥DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=①+②=0\), that is, BA⊥AC.
Exercise  28767： Let HDGC be a parallelogram. D is the midpoint of BG. HA⊥AD and BD⊥DC. Prove that BA⊥AC.
\(\because \) D is the midpoint of BG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DG}=- \small\overrightarrow{DB}\).
\(\because \) HDGC is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DH}=\small\overrightarrow{DC} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}=\small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\).
\(\because \) BD⊥DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) HA⊥AD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AH}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DH}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=①+②=0\), that is, BA⊥AC.
