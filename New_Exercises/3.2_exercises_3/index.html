Exercise  1： Let B, D, A be collinear and BD=2DA. B, F, A are collinear and FA=2BF. E is the midpoint of CA. BA=3CB. Prove that FE⊥ED.
\(\because \) B, D, A are collinear and BD=2DA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AD}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{3}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of CA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) B, F, A are collinear and FA=2BF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AF}=\dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{AB}}{3}\).
\(\because \) BA=3CB  \( \therefore\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2}}{9} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2}}{9} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{AB} - \small\overrightarrow{AC}\right)^{2}=- \dfrac{8 \small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2}}{9} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC} - \small\overrightarrow{AC}^{2}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{FE}=\left(- \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{AE} - \small\overrightarrow{AF}\right)=\left(- \dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{AB}}{3} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{3} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\right)=\dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2}}{9} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}^{2}}{4}=0\), that is, FE⊥ED.
