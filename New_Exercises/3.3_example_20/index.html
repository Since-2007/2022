Exercise  501： Let FABG be a cyclic quadrilateral, AB intersects FG at C. E is the midpoint of DF and BC. AC⊥CD. Prove that AG⊥BD.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of DF  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CF}=- \small\overrightarrow{CD} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\).
\(\because \) FABG is a cyclic quadrilateral, AB intersects FG at C  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=- \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CG} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG} - \small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) AC⊥CD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GA}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CG}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG} + \small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=-①-②=0\), that is, AG⊥BD.
Exercise  532： Let BDEC be a parallelogram. AEGD is a cyclic quadrilateral, AD intersects EG at C. AC⊥CB. Prove that AG⊥DB.
\(\because \) BDEC is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\).
\(\because \) AEGD is a cyclic quadrilateral, AD intersects EG at C  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \small\overrightarrow{CE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \small\overrightarrow{CG} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG} - \small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) AC⊥CB  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GA}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CG}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG} + \small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=-①-②=0\), that is, AG⊥DB.
Exercise  547： Let E be the midpoint of FB and DC. GA⊥DB and DC⊥CG. Given that G, B, C are collinear and F, C, A are collinear, prove that BC\(\cdot\)GC=FC\(\cdot\)CA.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CD}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of FB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CF}=- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CE}=- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\).
\(\because \) DC⊥CG  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) GA⊥DB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GA}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CG}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG} + \small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CF} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right) + \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=- \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CG}=①-②=0\)
\(\because\) G, B, C are collinear and F, C, A are collinear   \(\therefore\) BC\(\cdot\)GC=FC\(\cdot\)CA.
