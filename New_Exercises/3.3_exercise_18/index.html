Exercise  28： Let E be the midpoint of BC. BA⊥DE and BC⊥CA. Prove that \(DA^{2}=CA^{2} + DB^{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\).
\(\because \) BC⊥CA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BA⊥DE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CE}\right)=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}\right) \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{CB} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=-①+2\cdot②=0\), that is, \(DA^{2}=CA^{2} + DB^{2}\).
Exercise  30： Let E be the midpoint of BC. BC⊥CA. DA=BD. Given that BA//ED, prove that \(CA^{2}=2 BA \cdot ED\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) DA=BD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{AB} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BC⊥CA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB}=- \small\overrightarrow{AC} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{AB} - \small\overrightarrow{AC}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC} + \small\overrightarrow{AC}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(- 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE} - \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}\right) - \small\overrightarrow{AC}^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right) - \small\overrightarrow{AC}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD} - \small\overrightarrow{AC}^{2}=-①-②=0\)
\(\because\) BA//ED   \(\therefore\) \(CA^{2}=2 BA \cdot ED\).
Exercise  33： Let E be the midpoint of BC. BA⊥AC. DA=BD. Prove that BA⊥DE.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) DA=BD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{AB} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BA⊥AC  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=1/2\cdot①+1/2\cdot②=0\), that is, BA⊥DE.
Exercise  35： Let EBAC be a rectangle. F is the midpoint of BC. DA=BD. Prove that BA⊥FD.
\(\because \) EBAC is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE}=\small\overrightarrow{AB} + \small\overrightarrow{AC}\).
\(\because \) F is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AF}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) DA=BD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{AB} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) EC⊥CA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CE}=- \small\overrightarrow{AC} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AC} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DF}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AF}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=1/2\cdot①+1/2\cdot②=0\), that is, BA⊥FD.
Exercise  37： Let E be the midpoint of FA and BC. FC⊥CA. DA=BD. Prove that BA⊥ED.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of FA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AF}=2 \small\overrightarrow{AE}=\small\overrightarrow{AB} + \small\overrightarrow{AC}\).
\(\because \) DA=BD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{AB} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) FC⊥CA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CF}=- \small\overrightarrow{AC} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AC} + \small\overrightarrow{AF}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DE}=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{AD} + \small\overrightarrow{AE}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{AD}\right)=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB}^{2}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AC}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{AB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{AD}=1/2\cdot①+1/2\cdot②=0\), that is, BA⊥ED.
