Exercise  5952： Let FEDC be a trapezoid with FC//ED and FC=2ED. FGBN is a trapezoid with GB//FN and GB=2FN. E, H are the midpoints of AN, BC, respectively. BD⊥DC. BC=2AD. Prove that GC⊥HA.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of AN  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CN}}{2}\).
\(\because \) FC//ED and FC=2ED  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CF}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{CD} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\small\overrightarrow{CA} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CN}\).
\(\because \) GB//FN and GB=2FN  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CG}=\small\overrightarrow{CB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CF} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{CN}=2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} - 4 \small\overrightarrow{CD}\).
\(\because \) H is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CH}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\).
\(\because \) BC=2AD  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BD⊥DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=\small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{CG} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{HA}=\small\overrightarrow{CG} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CH}\right)=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CB} - 4 \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=2 \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - 4 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=-2\cdot①-2\cdot②=0\), that is, GC⊥HA.
Exercise  6144： Let ECBN be a trapezoid with CB//EN and CB=2EN. D, F are the orthocenter and circumcenter of △NCA. G is the midpoint of CB. CD⊥DB. CB=2DA. Prove that EF⊥GA.
\(\because \) CB//EN and CB=2EN  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DE}=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DN}\).
\(\because \) D, F are the orthocenter and circumcenter of △NCA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DF}=\small\overrightarrow{DA} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DN}\).
\(\because \) G is the midpoint of CB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DG}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2}\).
\(\because \) CB=2DA  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + \dfrac{\left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}}{4}=- \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}}{4} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}}{4}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) CD⊥DB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{EF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{GA}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DG}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{DE} + \small\overrightarrow{DF}\right)=\left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}}{4} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}}{4}=-①-②=0\), that is, EF⊥GA.
Exercise  13691： Let EGAF be a parallelogram. F, E are the midpoints of NA, BC, respectively. NE⊥EA and BD⊥DC. BC=2DA. Prove that GD⊥EA.
\(\because \) E is the midpoint of BC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CE}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\).
\(\because \) F is the midpoint of NA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CF}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CN}}{2}\).
\(\because \) EGAF is a parallelogram  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CG}=\small\overrightarrow{CA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CF}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CN}}{2}\).
\(\because \) BC=2DA  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{AD}^{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4} - \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CA} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4} - \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BD⊥DC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}=\small\overrightarrow{CD} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CB} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CD}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
\(\because \) NE⊥EA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{EA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EN}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CE}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CE} + \small\overrightarrow{CN}\right)=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CN}\right)=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CN} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CN}}{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(③\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{DG} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EA}=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \small\overrightarrow{CE}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{CD} + \small\overrightarrow{CG}\right)=\left(\small\overrightarrow{CA} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}}{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CN}}{2}\right)=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CB}}{4} - \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CN}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2}}{4} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CD}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{CB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{CN}}{4}=-1/2\cdot①-1/2\cdot②-1/2\cdot③=0\), that is, GD⊥EA.
