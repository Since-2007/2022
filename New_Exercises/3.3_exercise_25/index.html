Exercise  1： Let ADBC be a trapezoid with DB//AC and DB=2AC. F is the midpoint of EC. AB=AC. Given that D, E, B are collinear and A, F, B are collinear, prove that DE\(\cdot\)EB=4\(\cdot\)AF\(\cdot\)FB.
\(\because \) DB//AC and DB=2AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC}\).
\(\because \) F is the midpoint of EC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BF}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) AB=AC  \( \therefore\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(4 \small\overrightarrow{AF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF} + \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EB}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right) + 4 \small\overrightarrow{BF} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right) + 4 \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right)=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}=0\)
\(\because\) D, E, B are collinear and A, F, B are collinear   \(\therefore\) DE\(\cdot\)EB=4\(\cdot\)AF\(\cdot\)FB.
Exercise  3： Let DBEA be a trapezoid with BE//DA and BE=2DA. F is the midpoint of CE. AB=CA. Given that A, F, B are collinear and CD//BE, prove that \(BE·CD=2·AF·FB\).
\(\because \) BE//DA and BE=2DA  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}=\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) F is the midpoint of CE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BF}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) AB=CA  \( \therefore\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(2 \small\overrightarrow{AF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF} + \small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EB}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right) + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BF} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right) + 2 \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}}{2}=0\)
\(\because\) A, F, B are collinear and CD//BE   \(\therefore\) \(BE·CD=2·AF·FB\).
Exercise  12： Let AEFC be a trapezoid with EF//AC and EF=2AC. D is the midpoint of EC. A, D, B are collinear and \(AB^{2}=4 AD \cdot DB\). Given that E, B, F are collinear, prove that \(AC^{2}=BF \cdot EB\).
\(\because \) D is the midpoint of EC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) EF//AC and EF=2AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BF}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\).
\(\because \) A, D, B are collinear and \(AB^{2}=4 AD \cdot DB\)  \( \therefore\small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2}}{4}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2}}{4} + \small\overrightarrow{BD} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2}}{4} + \left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right)=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2}}{4} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}}{4} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}}{4}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{EB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{FB}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right) - \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE} - \small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}=0\)
\(\because\) E, B, F are collinear   \(\therefore\) \(AC^{2}=BF \cdot EB\).
Exercise  15： Let FEBC be a trapezoid with EB//FC and EB=2FC. D is the midpoint of CE. AD⊥DB. AB=AC. Prove that FA⊥EB.
\(\because \) D is the midpoint of CE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) EB//FC and EB=2FC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BF}=\small\overrightarrow{BC} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) AB=AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) AD⊥DB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BD}=\small\overrightarrow{BD} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)=\left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right)=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}}{4} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}}{4}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{AF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EB}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BC} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE} - \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}}{2}=-1/2\cdot①-2\cdot②=0\), that is, FA⊥EB.

Exercise  27： Let A, D, B be collinear and DB=2AD. F is the midpoint of CE. DE⊥EB and AF⊥FB. AB=AC. Prove that EB⊥CD.
\(\because \) A, D, B are collinear and DB=2AD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BD}=\dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{BA}}{3}\).
\(\because \) F is the midpoint of CE  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BF}=\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\).
\(\because \) AB=AC  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{CA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{BA} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) DE⊥EB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{DE} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EB}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BD} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{BA}}{3} + \small\overrightarrow{BE}\right)=\dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{3} - \small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
\(\because \) AF⊥FB  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{AF} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BF}=\small\overrightarrow{BF} \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \small\overrightarrow{BF}\right)=\left(\dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right) \cdot \left(- \small\overrightarrow{BA} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2}\right)=- \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BC}}{2} - \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC}^{2}}{4} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{2} + \dfrac{\small\overrightarrow{BE}^{2}}{4}=0\) . . . . . . \(③\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{DC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{EB}=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{BC} - \small\overrightarrow{BD}\right)=- \small\overrightarrow{BE} \cdot \left(- \dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{BA}}{3} + \small\overrightarrow{BC}\right)=\dfrac{2 \small\overrightarrow{BA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}}{3} - \small\overrightarrow{BC} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{BE}=-1/2\cdot①-1/2\cdot②-2\cdot③=0\), that is, EB⊥CD.
