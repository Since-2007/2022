Exercise  1： Let CA//BD and \(CD^{2}=2 BD \cdot CA\). Prove that \(BA^{2}=AD^{2} + CB^{2}\).
\(\because \) CA//BD and \(CD^{2}=2 BD \cdot CA\)  \( \therefore- 2 \small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right) - \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=- 2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} + 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=0.\)
In conclusion,  \(- \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right)^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC} + \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=0\), that is, \(BA^{2}=AD^{2} + CB^{2}\).
Exercise  5： Let BC=CD and AD=BA. Prove that AC⊥BD.
\(\because \) AD=BA  \(\therefore - \small\overrightarrow{BA}^{2} + \small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DA}^{2} - \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}\right)^{2}=2 \small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2}=0\) . . . . . . \(①\)
\(\because \) BC=CD  \(\therefore \small\overrightarrow{CB}^{2} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2}=- \small\overrightarrow{DC}^{2} + \left(\small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)^{2}=\small\overrightarrow{DB}^{2} - 2 \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=0\) . . . . . . \(②\)
In conclusion,  \(\small\overrightarrow{CA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB}=\small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \left(\small\overrightarrow{DA} - \small\overrightarrow{DC}\right)=\small\overrightarrow{DA} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DB} - \small\overrightarrow{DB} \cdot \small\overrightarrow{DC}=1/2\cdot①+1/2\cdot②=0\), that is, AC⊥BD.
